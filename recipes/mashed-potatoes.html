Instant Pot Leek Mashed Potatoes

Description

Instant Pot Leek Mashed Potatoes elevate the classic comfort food dish with the addition of savory leeks, offering a delightful twist on traditional mashed potatoes. This recipe harnesses the convenience and efficiency of the Instant Pot, allowing for quicker cooking times without sacrificing flavor or texture. The leeks impart a mild onion-like flavor and subtle sweetness, complementing the creamy richness of the mashed potatoes. Combined with butter, cream, and seasonings, these mashed potatoes achieve a luxurious creaminess and depth of flavor that is sure to tantalize the taste buds.

Preparing Instant Pot Leek Mashed Potatoes is a breeze, making it an ideal side dish for busy weeknight dinners or special occasions. The Instant Pot streamlines the cooking process by effortlessly steaming the potatoes and leeks to tender perfection in a fraction of the time it would take using traditional methods. Once cooked, the ingredients are mashed together to create a velvety smooth consistency, with the leeks adding specks of green for visual appeal. Whether served alongside roasted meats, grilled vegetables, or enjoyed on its own, Instant Pot Leek Mashed Potatoes offer a comforting and satisfying addition to any meal, promising to become a new favorite in your culinary repertoire.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound potatoes, peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 leek, white parts only, quartered lengthwise and finely sliced
  • ½ teaspoon coarse salt
  • water to cover
  • ⅓ cup milk, warmed
  • >2 tablespoons butter
  • salt to taste

Steps

  1. Combine potatoes, leek, and salt in a multi-functional pressure cooker (such as Instant Pot). Add water to cover, about 2 cups. Close and lock the lid. Select high pressure according to manufacturer's instructions; set timer for 10 minutes. Allow about 10 minutes for pressure to build.
  2. Release pressure using the natural-release method according to manufacturer's instructions, about 10 minutes. Carefully release remaining pressure using the quick-release method, about 5 minutes. Unlock and remove the lid. Drain potatoes well and return them to the pot.
  3. Select Saute function. Toss potatoes until dried and starting to sizzle, about 3 minutes. Add butter; stir until melted. Add milk. Mash potatoes with the back of a wooden spoon for a rustic mash, or run them through a ricer for a smoother mash. Taste and adjust the salt.
